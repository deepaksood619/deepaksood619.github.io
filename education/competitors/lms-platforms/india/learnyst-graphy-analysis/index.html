Learnyst vs Graphy: India LMS Comparison

Category: India B2B/B2C Creator LMS | Last updated: 2026-06-09

Executive Summary​

Learnyst and Graphy (formerly Spayee) are the India-native equivalents of Teachable/Thinkific — white-label LMS platforms for individual educators, coaches, and small coaching institutes to sell courses online. Both serve the gap that Teachable/Kajabi/Thinkific leave open: India-native payments, INR pricing, GST invoicing, regional language support.

Learnyst​

Overview​

  • Founded: 2015 | HQ: Bengaluru, India
  • Funding: Bootstrapped / seed-stage (not publicly disclosed)
  • Market: 3,000+ educators using the platform

Core Value Proposition​

"The most secure LMS to sell courses online from your own brand" — security and anti-piracy is the lead message, reflecting the real pain of Indian content creators: video piracy is rampant.

Strengths​

  • Built specifically for India's competitive exam market (JEE, NEET, UPSC, banking)
  • DRM + watermarking as lead feature (real need in India)
  • Test series + course combo in one platform (rare globally)
  • INR pricing with Indian payment gateway integration
  • GST invoicing support
  • Regional language support (Hindi, Tamil, Telugu, etc.)

Weaknesses​

  • Small team — slow feature development
  • No adaptive learning or personalization
  • Weak analytics compared to global platforms
  • Community features are basic
  • Not well-known outside India's coaching circle
  • Student cap on lower pricing tiers

Graphy (formerly Spayee)​

Overview​

  • Spayee founded: 2019 | Rebranded to Graphy
  • Acquired by: Unacademy (acquired Spayee to build white-label infra for educators leaving Unacademy)
  • Target: Individual creators, influencers, online educators who want branded course platforms

Background​

Spayee was a Teachable alternative built for India. Unacademy acquired it to power their "Go-to-Market for Educators" strategy — giving teachers who left Unacademy a platform to build their own brand. Rebranded as Graphy with expanded features.

Features​

Strengths​

  • Unacademy connection gives distribution muscle
  • WhatsApp integration is uniquely India-relevant
  • Influencer creator focus — simpler than Learnyst
  • Good for digital product creators, not just course educators
  • Affordable India pricing

Weaknesses​

  • Unacademy's own troubled finances (acquired by upGrad March 2026) create uncertainty
  • Less focus on exam/test series niche vs. Learnyst
  • Brand confusion from Spayee → Graphy rebrand
  • Unclear roadmap post-Unacademy acquisition by upGrad

Startup Implications​

Partially competitive — if we enable educators/coaches to build on our platform, we compete with Learnyst/Graphy for their business.

Lessons:

  1. India-native payments (Razorpay, PayU, UPI) and GST invoicing are non-negotiable for India market
  2. DRM/piracy protection is a genuine top-3 feature request from Indian educators
  3. WhatsApp is India's OS — any platform must integrate with it
  4. Exam/test series is a premium niche that global platforms don't serve
  5. India pricing must be 5-10x cheaper than global equivalents (₹1,000-5,000/month vs $50-400/month)

Gap we can exploit:

  • Neither Learnyst nor Graphy tracks learning outcomes or helps learners get jobs
  • They sell to educators (the supply), not to learners (the demand)
  • No adaptive learning — one-size-fits-all course structure
  • No placement data or career outcome tracking
  • India creator LMS is solving content distribution, not learning effectiveness
