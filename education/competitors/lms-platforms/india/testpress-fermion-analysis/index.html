Testpress & Fermion LMS Analysis

Comprehensive Competitor Analysis | Last updated: 2026-06-09

Executive Summary​

  • Category: B2B LMS & EdTech Infrastructure / White-Label Assessment Platform
  • Testpress Founded: 2014 | Location: Chennai, India
  • Fermion Founded: ~2022 | Acquired by Testpress: 2025
  • Scale: 1,000+ institutes, 20L+ students (Testpress); hundreds of education businesses (Fermion)
  • Team Size: 44 employees (March 2026, up 26% YoY)
  • Funding: Bootstrapped (no external funding)
  • Business Model: B2B SaaS — white-label LMS sold to coaching institutes, test-prep centers, ed-tech companies, corporates

Competitive Advantages (Combined Entity):

  • Only India-built LMS combining proctored exams + live interactive classes + coding sandbox in one platform
  • 10+ years of product refinement serving Indian coaching institute market (Testpress)
  • AI-native live class infrastructure with in-browser code execution (from Fermion)
  • DRM video protection on par with Netflix/Amazon Prime (Google Widevine + Apple FairPlay)
  • Bootstrapped — no investor pressure, customer-funded growth
  • White-label capability: institutes keep their own brand

Weaknesses / Risks:

  • Small team (44 people) trying to integrate two products post-acquisition
  • Founder of Fermion (Mehul Mohan) stepped into advisory role — key talent risk
  • Primarily India-focused coaching institute market (limited international play so far)
  • No publicly visible pricing (opaque for enterprise)
  • Less brand recognition vs. funded competitors (Classplus, Teachmint)

Company Overview​

Testpress​

Founded: 2014

Founder: Dinesh Kumar (Coimbatore Institute of Technology, 2004–2008; previously at MontaVista Software)

Headquarters: Chennai, Tamil Nadu, India

Legal Entity: Testpress Tech Labs LLP

Core Mission: Give educational institutes everything needed to run a learning business in one place — courses, exams, videos, proctoring, and student management.

What they built over 10 years:

  • Online exam & assessment platform (origin product)
  • Full LMS for course delivery (video, PDFs, assignments)
  • TPStreams — proprietary video streaming with DRM
  • AI-assisted proctoring
  • White-label mobile + web apps for institutes

Customer Profile:

  • Coaching institutes & test-prep centers (JEE, NEET, UPSC)
  • Training institutes and bootcamps
  • Educational institutions (schools, colleges)
  • Corporate training departments
  • Notable partnerships: TATA, MTG

Fermion​

Founded: ~2022

Founder: Mehul Mohan — BITS Pilani, Goa (CS); previously founder of codedamn (coding practice platform, pre-seed funded by Antler India $175K in 2021)

Headquarters: Bengaluru → San Francisco (Mehul relocated; later reported burnout, moved to Dubai)

Core Mission: AI-ready cloud infrastructure for modern ed-tech businesses — particularly those teaching technical/coding skills requiring hands-on practice.

What they built:

  • Native live classes — no third-party dependency (Zoom, etc.), unlimited concurrent viewers
  • Coding sandbox / coding labs — in-browser code execution, arbitrary language support
  • AI-powered LMS — personalized learning paths, smart content delivery
  • Real-time recording — streams available for playback immediately after ending
  • Video streaming platform
  • API for arbitrary code execution (available on RapidAPI)

Customers at acquisition: Hundreds of education businesses (exact number undisclosed)

Exit context: Mehul reported burnout with operating a business in India; moved to Dubai. Acquisition provided an exit while keeping Fermion's product alive under Testpress.

The Acquisition — Strategic Rationale​

Announcement: Welcoming Fermion to the Testpress Family | Fermion's acquisition page

Why Testpress acquired Fermion:

Testpress customers were consistently requesting one missing piece: real-time, two-way live classes where students can talk, raise hands, and interact with the teacher. Building this natively from scratch would have taken significant time. Fermion had already built exactly that — plus a coding sandbox Testpress didn't have.

"When Testpress started, the goal was to give educators everything they need to run a learning business in one place, and our customers kept asking for the missing piece: real-time, two-way live classes where students can talk, raise hands, and interact with the teacher. Mehul and the Fermion team built exactly that." — Testpress blog

Why Fermion sold:

  • Founder burnout (Mehul Mohan publicly cited "completely burned out with India's system")
  • Relocation to Dubai meant operating from outside India
  • Joining a larger company (Testpress) allowed product and customers to continue with more engineering resources and support coverage

What changed post-acquisition:

  • Fermion's product continues operating; customers migrated to Testpress ecosystem
  • Mehul Mohan took an advisory role
  • Testpress gained: live interactive classes + coding labs + Fermion's customer base
  • Combined with TPStreams' broadcast live capabilities → full live + video stack in one platform

Key Differentiators vs Competitors​

vs. Classplus / Teachmint (funded India LMS):

  • Stronger assessment/exam capabilities (10 years of test-prep market focus)
  • Coding sandbox — unique in India LMS market
  • Bootstrapped = no pressure to pivot to consumer ed-tech

vs. Moodle / Canvas (global LMS):

  • India-first: supports Hindi, Tamil, Telugu, Kannada, Bengali
  • Proctoring built-in (not a plugin)
  • Localized payment gateways, pricing in INR

vs. Teachable / Thinkific (creator platforms):

  • B2B institute-grade (not solo creator focused)
  • Full exam infrastructure, not just video courses
  • Coding education support

Business Model​

Revenue model: B2B SaaS subscription

  • Pricing starts at ₹28,999/year (India market) or ~$50/month (SMB tier)
  • White-label licensing for institutes who want branded apps
  • Flexible course monetization: subscriptions, one-time purchase, bundles

Go-to-market: Direct sales to coaching institutes, partnerships (TATA, MTG), inbound via SEO

Bootstrapped growth: No external funding; 44 employees serving 1,000+ institutes — lean, customer-funded

Competitive Threat to Our Startup​

Direct overlap:

  • Any product targeting Indian coaching institutes, test-prep centers, or tech bootcamps with LMS + assessment needs will encounter Testpress
  • The Fermion acquisition makes Testpress more dangerous for platforms targeting coding education

Gap they still don't fill well:

  • Adaptive learning algorithms (personalized content sequencing beyond AI Q&A)
  • Spaced repetition / cognitive science-based learning
  • Student career outcomes tracking / placement support
  • Consumer-facing brand (they are B2B infrastructure)
  • Community features, peer learning, cohort collaboration

Our positioning advantage:

  • If targeting end-learners (not institutes), Testpress is a back-end infrastructure play — they don't compete on learner experience or outcomes
  • Adaptive AI personalization is not their core (they add AI to existing product vs. AI-first design)
  • Placement and job-outcome focus is outside their scope

Key Signals to Monitor​

  • When Fermion's live class tech is fully integrated into Testpress LMS (timeline TBD)
  • Whether Testpress raises external funding to accelerate growth
  • International expansion plans (currently India-centric)
  • AI roadmap beyond proctoring and doubt resolution
  • Whether Mehul Mohan re-engages or fully exits (talent continuity risk)
  • Competing acquisitions (Classplus, Teachmint may make similar moves)
