## Business Model

### Core Value Proposition: Mentor Network Referrals

**Verified Structure (High Confidence):**

Preplaced operates a **mentorship-first placement model** combining:

1. **1:1 Mentorship Sessions**
   - 4-8 live sessions on average (per mentee)
   - Frequency: "frequent One on One sessions"
   - Format: Video calls with assigned mentor

2. **Unlimited Chat Support**
   - "Doubt? Get the right advice from your mentor via Chat"
   - Asynchronous communication channel

3. **Task Assignments + Curated Resources**
   - Structured learning tasks from mentor
   - Resource library (articles, videos, practice problems)

4. **Certification**
   - Completion certificate upon finishing mentorship program

5. **Job Referrals** (Core Differentiator)
   - "Get Referrals from mentor community to top product and service based companies"
   - Mentor networks used for direct referrals to hiring teams
   - No explicit placement guarantee (unlike Masai School)

**Source:** preplaced.in (June 2026)

**Evidence Quality:** ✅ High - Direct verification from official website, core features listed explicitly

### Payment Model & Risk Mitigation

**Verified:**

- **7-day money-back guarantee** - indicates outcome depends on mentee effort, not platform guarantee
- **Pricing:** Not disclosed on public website (likely varies by domain/mentor)

**Implication:** Platform positions as enabler (mentor access + referrals), not outcome guarantor. Student effort drives results.

## Unverified Marketing Claims ❌

**CRITICAL:** The following claims appeared on Preplaced's website but **failed adversarial verification**:

### Outcome Claims (0-3 vote, strongly refuted)

- ❌ "70% of mentees achieve their career aspirations on average"
- ❌ "Compared to a 30% success rate for people using other methods"
- ❌ "50% faster results (6 months instead of years)"

**Why Refuted (0-3 vote):** Self-reported success metrics with no methodology disclosure, no control group, no independent verification. Claims like "career aspirations" are subjectively defined. Comparison to "other methods" lacks specificity (which methods? measured how?).

### Cost-Effectiveness Claims (0-3 vote, strongly refuted)

- ❌ "30% cheaper than 6-month courses"
- ❌ "4x better results"
- ❌ "90% consistency vs 10% course completion rate"

**Why Refuted (0-3 vote):** No evidence of comparative pricing analysis, no definition of "better results," completion rate claim (90% vs 10%) is implausibly large gap suggesting cherry-picked comparison.

### Mentor Network Claims (1-2 vote, refuted)

- ❌ "Network of 600+ mentors from major tech companies including Google, Amazon, Meta, Apple"
- ❌ "Frontend: 27+ mentors, Backend: 120+ mentors, Data Science: 48+ mentors, Product Management: 32+ mentors"

**Why Refuted (1-2 vote):** Could not verify mentor roster independently. LinkedIn profiles of claimed mentors not cross-referenced. Numbers may include inactive/past mentors or conflate advisors with active mentors.

## Competitive Positioning

### vs. Bootcamps (Masai School, Scaler, upGrad)

| Dimension | Preplaced (Mentorship) | Bootcamps |
|-----------|----------------------|-----------|
| Learning Model | 1:1 mentorship (4-8 sessions) | Cohort-based curriculum (12-24 weeks) |
| Curriculum | Mentor-directed tasks | Structured syllabus |
| Placement Mechanism | Mentor network referrals | Hiring partnerships + placement teams |
| Placement Guarantee | None (7-day refund only) | Varies (Masai: yes, others: no) |
| Duration | Flexible (mentor-dependent) | Fixed (6-12 months) |
| Scale | Limited by mentor availability | Cohort-based (100s-1000s per batch) |

**Preplaced Advantage:** Personalized guidance, flexible pacing, potentially faster (6 months claim).

**Bootcamp Advantage:** Systematic curriculum, peer learning, explicit placement guarantees (Masai).

### vs. Pure Job Boards (LinkedIn Jobs, Naukri)

| Feature | Preplaced | Pure Job Board |
|---------|-----------|---------------|
| Learning/Upskilling | Yes (mentor-guided) | No (job matching only) |
| Referrals | Mentor network (warm intros) | Cold applications |
| Preparation Support | 1:1 mentorship | None (DIY) |
| Placement Assistance | Referrals (no guarantee) | None (platform facilitates, doesn't assist) |

**Preplaced Advantage:** Combines upskilling + warm referrals (vs. cold applications).

**Job Board Advantage:** Free access, larger company database, no time commitment.

### vs. Mentorship Platforms (ADPList, MentorCruise)

| Dimension | Preplaced | General Mentorship Platforms |
|-----------|-----------|----------------------------|
| Goal | Job placement (career transition) | Skill development, career advice |
| Referrals | Core feature (community referrals) | Rare (mentor-dependent, not systematic) |
| Structure | 4-8 sessions + tasks + cert | Highly variable (mentor-defined) |
| Job Outcome Focus | High (marketing emphasizes placement) | Low (mentorship for growth, not jobs) |

**Preplaced Advantage:** Placement-focused, structured program, referral guarantee.

**General Platform Advantage:** Broader mentor pool, lower cost (some free mentorship).

## Strategic Implications for Our Startup

### What Preplaced's Model Reveals

1. **Mentorship Has Market Demand**
   - Preplaced builds entire business model around 1:1 mentor relationships
   - Students value personalized guidance, not just content/curriculum
   - Question: Can AI mentorship replicate human mentor value, or is it complementary?

2. **Warm Referrals > Cold Applications**
   - Core value prop is mentor network access for job referrals
   - Suggests job seekers struggle with cold application conversion
   - Implication: Our platform needs hiring partner network OR mentor referral system

3. **Outcome Metrics Are Inflated (Industry-Wide)**
   - 70% success, 4x better results, 90% consistency - all refuted
   - Similar to Scaler (78% transition rate refuted), Masai (40k learners refuted)
   - Industry norm: aggressive self-reporting without third-party verification

4. **Mentor Model Has Scaling Constraints**
   - 1:1 mentorship caps growth at mentor availability
   - Unlike bootcamps (1 instructor : 30 students) or MOOCs (1 course : unlimited)
   - Question: Can AI scale mentorship while preserving personalization?

### Questions for Our Model

1. **AI Mentorship vs. Human Mentorship?**
   - Can adaptive AI provide mentor-level guidance (personalized feedback, task assignment, doubt resolution)?
   - Where does human mentor add irreplaceable value (referrals, industry insight, emotional support)?
   - Hybrid model: AI for curriculum, human mentors for career guidance + referrals?

2. **Referral Networks vs. Direct Hiring Partnerships?**
   - Preplaced: Mentor community referrals (informal, relationship-driven)
   - Masai/Scaler: Direct hiring partnerships (formal, volume-driven)
   - Which scales better? Which works for adaptive learning platform?

3. **Certification Value?**
   - Preplaced offers completion certificate (no disclosed employer recognition)
   - Is certificate sufficient, or do we need university/industry-recognized credentials?

4. **How Many Sessions Are Sufficient?**
   - Preplaced: 4-8 sessions average
   - Is this enough for career transition, or is it supplementary to self-learning?
   - If AI handles curriculum, can 4-8 human sessions focus purely on job search strategy?
